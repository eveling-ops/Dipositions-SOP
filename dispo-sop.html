Pricing approval
Step 5 is a hard stop

Nothing goes to buyers until Chris signs off on both the starting price and ARV. Steps 1–4 are prep. Steps 6–11 are outreach. The gate is non-negotiable.

Buyer list sourcing logic
Which list to use

Always check the existing buyer database first:

  • Filter for buyers who have purchased in the property's county
  • Filter for buyers who have purchased in any county within 50 miles
  • Only if no matches → pull a fresh list via InvestorBase
GHL campaign setup
GoHighLevel checklist
  • Log into the Disposition sub-account in GHL — not the main account
  • Duplicate an existing SMS + email workflow — never build from scratch
  • Update the initial text message and email action items with this property's details
  • Upload the buyer list and name it by the property address
  • Launch the workflow to fire both campaigns simultaneously
Buyer response handling
DispoGPT replies

For every interested buyer response across InvestorLift, SMS, or email:

  • Copy and paste the buyer's message into DispoGPT
  • Use the generated response as your reply
  • Tag the buyer in the tracking sheet immediately after first contact
Email subject line prompt
Use this prompt to generate subject lines

"I need engaging email subject lines for an off-market property we're selling. Here's the property information: [Insert Property Information]. Please generate 5 subject lines that are short, enticing, and highlight only positive attributes. Make them professional yet compelling and encourage buyers to open the email."

Tracking sheet reminder
One sheet per deal

Create a new dedicated tracking sheet for each property — do not reuse a previous deal's sheet. Name it by property address. Log every interested buyer, offer, conversation, and follow-up note there.
